Club History:

Chelsea Football Club, founded in 1905, is an English professional football club based in London. The club has experienced both ups and downs throughout its history but has emerged as a force in modern football.

In 2003, Chelsea underwent a significant transformation when it was purchased by Russian billionaire Roman Abramovich. The club's financial backing enabled them to assemble a star-studded team and achieve remarkable success.

Under the guidance of managers such as Jose Mourinho, Carlo Ancelotti, and Antonio Conte, Chelsea won numerous domestic and international titles, including multiple Premier League titles, FA Cups, League Cups, and a UEFA Champions League triumph in 2012.

Shirt Sponsor:

Chelsea FC has had several notable shirt sponsors throughout its history. From 1983 to 1993, the club's shirts featured the logo of Commodore, a computer company. Following that, Chelsea struck a deal with Coors, an American brewing company, as their shirt sponsor from 1993 to 2001. The following year, the club signed a record-breaking sponsorship deal with mobile phone company, Emirates, which lasted until 2005.

Then, Samsung became Chelsea's shirt sponsor from 2005 to 2015. Since 2015, the club has partnered with Yokohama Rubber Company, a Japanese tire manufacturer. This ongoing sponsorship deal has seen the Yokohama logo prominently displayed on the front of Chelsea's shirts.
